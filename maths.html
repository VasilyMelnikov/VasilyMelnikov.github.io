Galois cohomology in Kummer theory
Kummer theory establishes a characterization of field extensions which adjunct an \(n\)-th root of a given element of the original field. I establish this basic result of Kummer theory using Galois cohomology, a cohomology theory for Galois modules.

The Riemann hypothesis for function fields
Many of the tools of modern algebraic geometry were developed to solve the Weil conjectures, which are statements about the zeta function of an algebraic variety over a finite field. In particular, they assert the zeta function is rational, solves a certain functional equation, and satisfies an analogue of the Riemann hypothesis. This article will examine and prove the third.

Operator algebraic approach to topology
Geometric objects are often studied by considering algebras of functions. In fact, the two approaches are often equivalent. In this blog post, I examine one such equivalence: a duality between commutative \(C^{\ast}\)-algebras and locally compact Hausdorff spaces. I then prove a result, first due to I. Gelfand and M. Naimark, connecting \(C^{\ast}\)-algebras to Hilbert spaces. This emphasizes the idea that the study of Hilbert spaces is in some sense a generalization of classical geometry.

Cylinder set measures in mathematical physics
Assuming familiarity with L. Gross's theory of abstract Wiener spaces and the Cameron-Martin quasi-invariance theorem, I present an application of the aforementioned theory to mathematical physics. In particular, a rigorous interpretation of a special case of R. Feynman's path integral is given.
